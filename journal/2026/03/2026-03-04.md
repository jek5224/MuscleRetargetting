## Systematic DOF grid baking for lower-body muscles

Built `tools/bake_dof_grid.py` — replaces BVH-based training data extraction with systematic joint angle sampling.

**Rationale**: Lower body has only 7 DOFs per leg (hip 3 ball, knee 1 revolute, ankle 3 ball). Systematic sampling gives better ROM coverage than 82 BVH files with far less compute and no redundancy.

**ROM ranges** (radians, from CDC/biomechanics + observed BVH):
- Hip: flex [-30°, 120°], rot [-45°, 45°], ab/ad [-45°, 30°]
- Knee: [0°, 140°]
- Ankle: x [-20°, 20°], y [-30°, 30°], z [-20°, 50°]

**Sampling**:
- L_UpLeg (4 DOFs): full grid, 10/DOF = 10,000 poses (~2 hours at 0.7s/sample)
- L_LowLeg (7 DOFs): LHS, 5K-10K samples (grid would explode to 10M+)
- Auto mode picks grid vs LHS based on DOF count

**Test**: 16 samples (2/DOF grid) ran in 11s, 0.7s/sample. Output verified: per-muscle NPZ chunks + DOF values + meta.json.

## V3 DOF-based NN training pipeline

Built training pipeline for deterministic DOF→deformation mapping:

**Architecture** (reuses DistillNetV2 with larger config):
- Input: 7 raw DOFs (hip x/y/z, knee, ankle x/y/z) — no derivatives needed
- Hidden: 1024, Encoder: 6 ResBlocks, Decoder: 4 ResBlocks
- Muscle embedding: 128D, Positional encoding: 8 freq bands
- Output: 64 PCA coefficients per muscle (37 muscles total)
- No temporal loss, no dropout — overfit to deterministic mapping

**Files**:
- `volume_distill/preprocess_dof_grid.py` — loads both regions, pelvis-local transform, PCA
- `volume_distill/train_dof_grid.py` — 1000 epochs, cosine LR, TensorBoard logging
- Updated `evaluate.py` and viewer for v3_dof checkpoint loading + 7-DOF inference

**Workflow**: bake finishes → `python -m volume_distill.preprocess_dof_grid` → `python -m volume_distill.train_dof_grid`
